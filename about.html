About Us

Initiative for Gender Equity and Sensitisation (IGES) at IIT Delhi aims to develop a model, where the unit will undertake gender sensitization activities and will also, identify opportunities to reduce gender gaps in STEM disciplines and to co-facilitate the gender mainstreaming exercise with the support of the Institutes administration.

It calls for integrating social responsibility into scientific and engineering disciplines and looks forward to strengthen institutional bureaucracy to be gender-sensitive. Therefore the IGES team calls for a robust team led by the executive committee with faculty members, staff members, student representatives, from diverse fields (science, engineering and social sciences)and diverse skills, with collective interest, sensitivity towards gender and sexual diversities and commitment to implement strategies to deal with gender gaps and discrimination, to achieve the above-mentioned goals.

IGES Mandate
  • Encouraging Gender Dialogues in the field of STEM
  • Advocating a zero-tolerance policy against sexual harassment
  • Creating an equal, inclusive and gender-just educational environment

    IGES founded in 2018, succeeded the Gender Awareness Committee (GAC), founded and led by two gender-sensitive and active female faculty members at IIT Delhi - Prof. Ravinder Kaur (present IGES Convenor) and Prof. Sangeeta Kohli (present IIT Delhi ICC Chairperson, ex-IGES Core Committee member) who are also the founding members of IGES, with other faculty members from science and engineering fields.

    Amongst the various responsibilities of the founding members, a significant one was the nomination of the IGES core committee members, who are sensitive and keen to institutionalise strategies to integrate gender diversity and address concerns across the IIT community; and appointing two external experts as women Program Officers, who are responsible for strategizing and visioning IGES role within IIT Delhi. The diverse composition of the core team has helped IGES members to develop a holistic understanding on the issues and strategic needs of different members of the institute. It has helped to co-own, co-learn and share responsibilities of mainstreaming gender sensitivity, gender justice and equity.

    IGES presently is attempting to design and develop a unique model in IIT Delhi, an amalgamation of science with social consciousness and responsibilities. It is still in an emerging state. New roles and strategies are being designed, adapted and piloted each day to build the roadmap for the unit, which later could also inspire IITs all over the country to adopt and adapt. Thus, its a learning journey for us all.
